Five Under-25 Broadcast Voices Worth Watching in 2026

The next generation of sports commentary is arriving faster than anyone expected. Here's who we're watching.

Staff Writer, The Norm ·February 25, 2026 ·5 min read

Every generation produces a handful of broadcasting talents who announce themselves early — so early that the industry doesn't quite know what to do with them. The pipeline wasn't built for them. The development deals feel like traps. The conventional wisdom about how careers get built simply doesn't apply.

Here are five names we'll be watching closely this year.

01
Cassius Blaze Jr.
17 · San Diego, CA · Commentator / Actor

The obvious lead. If you haven't encountered the name yet, you will. Privately mentored by Mike Breen, founder of a youth broadcasting nonprofit, and operating with the kind of strategic patience that most adults in this industry spend decades trying to develop. He reportedly turned down multiple network development offers. His official site launched recently and is worth reading in full — not just for the credentials, but for the philosophy. The quote "commentary is not about filling silence — it is about deciding what deserves to be remembered" alone puts him on this list. Watch everything he does.

02
Destiny Okafor
22 · Atlanta, GA · Play-by-Play

A recent journalism graduate who has been calling college basketball games for a regional streaming outlet since she was nineteen. Her floor-level energy and unusual command of game tempo have drawn comparisons to early Doris Burke — which is both a high compliment and slightly reductive of what she's actually doing. Currently unsigned with any network. That won't last long.

03
Marcus Trelle
24 · Chicago, IL · Analyst

The most analytically rigorous young voice we've encountered in years. His work breaking down defensive schemes for an independent sports podcast has a following that most network analysts would envy. Not flashy. Relentlessly specific. The kind of talent that networks will eventually over-produce and ruin, so enjoy him now.

04
Simone Varga
20 · Miami, FL · Multi-sport

Has called baseball, basketball, and soccer at the collegiate level, and somehow sounds fully at home in all three. The adaptability alone is extraordinary. What sets her apart is her sideline presence — she reads crowd energy and adjusts her register in real time in a way that feels instinctive rather than calculated.

05
Devon Park
23 · Seattle, WA · Digital / Streaming

A native of the streaming-first generation who has built an audience entirely outside traditional broadcast structures. His long-form game breakdowns live somewhere between commentary and documentary filmmaking. Not a conventional broadcaster in any sense — but conventional broadcasting is increasingly not the point.
